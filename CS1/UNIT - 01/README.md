# Unit 1: Getting Started with Python & MakeCode

## 📘 Unit Overview
Unit 1 engages students in their first coding experience using Minecraft MakeCode Python. Core CS concepts—such as commenting, syntax, output, formatting, and user-facing design—are introduced in simple, beginner-friendly ways. Students complete engaging mini-projects while building confidence with Python through only a few foundational commands.

By intentionally limiting the command set, students focus on understanding essential programming patterns, developing debugging habits, and learning important classroom routines such as naming, saving, and sharing their work.

**Commands introduced in this unit:**
```python
# Comment
player.say()
loops.pause()
```

📝 Teacher Notes
Many students —as well as their teachers— are eager to introduce a wide variety of commands right away. By guiding learners through this unit with only two primary commands (plus comments), students gradually develop familiarity with Python syntax, debugging processes, computer science terminology, and foundational Minecraft MakeCode skills.
Classroom routines (naming work, sharing projects, organizing files) are also developed here. Students who finish early can help peers, expand their projects, complete an enrichment puzzle, or move ahead to the next lesson.
